"So, tell me about yourself"
...I'm Shehab Ibrahim

A Human-Computer Interaction undergraduate student at New Jersey Institute of Technology. There, I conduct HCI research with a talented Ph.D. candidate that has helped me hone my skills in the realm of UX/UI/IX design. I have quickly become an advocate of the user and pride myself in my ability to make meaningful connections with "Human and Machine."
It took a while to get this far.

Before college, I was interested in all sorts of Multimedia. I joined the JCArts program in Jersey City, NJ and learned a good deal. I was even fortunate enough to take a Graphic Design course at the School of Visual Arts in New York City.

I’ve always known that I wanted to work in the Tech industry. Companies like Google, Amazon, and Microsoft make the news constantly, and it seemed like the place to be.

My moment of uncertainty started when I explained this dream to a high school teacher that I respected greatly. He expressed that I would be “stupid” to try and climb the “Tech Mountain” in a non-analytical field. The problem is, I agreed with him. I needed a field where my work wouldn’t be pushed to the side because of one-sided opinion or bias. I needed my work to stand its own ground.

This story would be over if I found out about UX… this isn’t where I found out about UX.

I was in the process of applying to college; I needed a major to which I would dedicate my entire life. With the dream of wanting to work in tech in mind, I decided Computer Science would be the most fitting.

I ended up enrolling at New Jersey Institute of Technology. I learned a lot in this field. I became very fluent in Python, Java, HTML, CSS and more. I maintained good grades, put on the dean's list, and made friends. However, the field seemed empty to me. I had minimal creative control of how other visualized my work. I was able to be creative in the back-end side of things, but not where it mattered- the front-end.

Throughout my awkward first semester fumbling through campus and meeting countless unfamiliar faces. I heard about someone talking about the HCI major- I was beyond curious.

I ended up enrolling in IS 247- Designing the User Experience. In that class, I learned about a field that went completely under my radar. I worked hard in that class, and the professor took notice. At the conclusion of the course, I was invited to conduct research within the Connections Lab of New Jersey Institute of Technology.

I still have a ways to go, but I’m content with how far I’m progressing.

I’ve presently accepted an offer to work with Prudential Financial as a User Experience Intern in the Summer of 2019.
