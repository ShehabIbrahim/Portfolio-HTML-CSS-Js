NJFit:
Augmenting Recreational Group Activities

As a member of the NJIT Connections Lab, I have been involved in researching the User Experience (UX) and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) concepts that have shaped me as an individual and a professional. I joined after completing a lengthy project that tested my aptitude for this type of work. I loved every minute of it and wanted to do it for a long time. Many projects and ideas are thrown across the room daily, and I have the privilege of working with some really great like-minded people. I've been working on topics ranging from Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) to Computer-Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW) and Ubiquitous Computing because I believe that the research being conducted in these areas will have a great impact on the future. As technologies are an essential part of how we presently communicate with one another, our research aims to transform the way through which we create social links. By participating in this lab, I have managed to learn how to research user needs and implement research findings in my design work.
